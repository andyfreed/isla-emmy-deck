# Isla & Emmy: Funky Islands — Lore & World

## Premise (Emmy's idea — CANON) 🌙
The 12 lucky Zodiac animals (Korean zodiac 띠) lived on the **Moon**. Someone did
something, and they all **fell from the Moon** onto the Funky Islands. Cut off from
the Moon they **lost their good luck**, turned grumpy, and started **causing trouble
in the village**. Isla & Emmy **calm each one** (a funky dance-off) and **send it
home to the Moon**. Calm all 12 to restore the good luck.

## The connective tissue (makes it all make sense — grounded in real folklore)

**Moonlight = "the Groove" = good luck.** The Moon is the Zodiac's home; its light
is the rhythm of good fortune. The 12 take turns riding a moonbeam down to bless
each year. So being knocked off the Moon = cut off from the Groove = no more luck.

**The villain is the Cat (real zodiac myth).** In the legendary Great Race that
chose the 12 signs, the **Rat tricked the Cat** so it missed the race — which is why
the Cat is NOT in the zodiac. In our story the Cat spent ages lonely and left-out on
Earth while the 12 got the Moon. Jealous and lonely, the **Cat bumps all 12 off the
Moon** — wanting their place, or just wanting them gone. A redeemable villain.

**Why they cause trouble:** without the Groove the 12 lose their luck AND their
rhythm — clumsy, grumpy, unlucky — so they accidentally wreck things (an unlucky Ox
stampedes, a luckless Rooster crows at 3am, a jinxed Snake tangles all the ropes…).

**Why Isla & Emmy:** each was **born under a zodiac sign** (Isla = Rat 쥐, Emmy =
Monkey 원숭이), so they still carry a spark of the Groove — the only ones who can
**dance the luck back** into a creature. Restoring a creature's funk = restoring its
luck, so it can leap home to the Moon. (This is why battles are rhythm dance-offs.)

## The arc (3 acts)
- **Act 1 — The Village (hub/tutorial):** trouble in town; calm the first fallen
  creature, learn the dance-off, earn the hot-air balloon.
- **Act 2 — The Islands:** balloon island to island calming the 12; each leaps home
  to the Moon as you restore its groove (and grants you its zodiac power). Glimpses
  of cat-eyes in the shadows hint at who's behind it.
- **Act 3 — The Moon (finale):** confront the Cat. Twist — it was never evil, just
  left out. Instead of beating it, the sisters give it a place to belong: the Cat
  becomes the honorary **13th friend**. Zodiac return, luck restored, big **full-moon
  festival** (Chuseok vibes) to celebrate. Loop.

## How it maps to mechanics
- 12 zodiac creatures = the enemies. Funk-Off battle = calming one (losable).
- Calm a creature -> it returns to the Moon AND leaves its zodiac power (its luck gift).
- Collect/send home all 12 -> beat the Cat (befriend) -> win.
- Sisters start with their birth-year guardian sign (Isla=Rat, Emmy=Monkey).

## The world
- **Home Village** (hub, built): scrolling organic island; shop, level-up, NPCs,
  balloon launch.
- **Hot-air balloon:** travels village -> islands where creatures cause trouble.
- **The Moon:** hangs above; where you send creatures home; the finale.
